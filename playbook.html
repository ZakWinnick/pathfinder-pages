Program Overview

Program Philosophy

Pathfinder Rewards transforms charging stops into a journey-based rewards experience. Like the best hotel loyalty programs, we focus on recognition, exclusive experiences, and making members feel valued - not just transactional discounts.

The hub-satellite network creates unique opportunities: Basecamps offer premium experiences, while Waystations provide seamless convenience, all connected through one loyalty ecosystem.

Core Positioning

"We're not rewarding you for charging your car. We're rewarding you for exploring America's scenic highways with us."

This positions Pathfinder Rewards as a travel lifestyle program that happens to include EV charging, rather than a charging network that happens to have a points program.

Key Differentiators

  1. Hospitality-grade loyalty - We learned from Marriott Bonvoy, not gas station rewards cards
  2. Experience-first benefits - Events, exclusive access, community over pure discounts
  3. Hub-satellite coordination - Unified member experience across all locations
  4. Invite-only elite tier - Trailblazer status creates aspirational goal
  5. Network effects - Benefits grow as our corridor coverage expands

Launch Strategy

Phase 1: Soft Launch (Months 1-3)

Timing: 3 months before first Basecamp opens

Tactics

  • Early bird pricing: Founding Pioneer @ $199/year (limit 500)
  • Pre-launch Explorer signups: Build email list, collect route preferences
  • Community seeding: Bay Area Rivian Club, NorCal EVs get early access codes
  • Digital presence: Launch rewards page on website (rangeway.app), social media campaign

Goal

1,000 Explorer signups, 150 Founding Pioneer members before opening

Key Deliverables

  • Rangeway.app website live
  • Mobile app beta (TestFlight for iOS, closed beta for Android)
  • Email automation sequences set up
  • Social media content calendar (3 months)
  • Founding Member certificate design
  • Early access codes generated

Phase 2: Grand Opening Activation (Month 3-6)

Timing: Aligned with first hub cluster opening (1 Basecamp + 2 Waystations)

Tactics

  • First 100 sessions free for new members (Explorers)
  • Basecamp launch event: Invite all early members, local EV clubs, press
  • Referral blitz: "Invite 3 friends, unlock bonus benefits"
  • Partnership announcements: Tourism board co-marketing, local business discounts

Goal

3,000 total members (35% conversion to paid tiers over 6 months)

Launch Day Checklist

  • All chargers operational and member recognition working
  • Staff trained on all 4 membership tiers
  • Basecamp caf√© stocked and ready
  • Pioneer merchandise available for purchase
  • Digital signage displaying member benefits
  • Press kit distributed to local media
  • Social media coverage plan activated
  • First 100 sessions tracking system active

Phase 3: Optimization & Expansion (Month 6-12)

Timing: Post-launch, refining based on data

Tactics

  • Tiered challenges: "Charge at all 3 sites this quarter, earn $50 credit"
  • Seasonal campaigns: "Summer Road Rally" exclusive routes for members
  • Partner ecosystem expansion: Add 5-10 new local partnerships per region
  • Pioneer Advisory Panel: Quarterly feedback sessions
  • First Trailblazer invitations: Identify and invite top 25 members

Goal

35% of charging sessions from paid members, 70 NPS from members

Phase 4: Network Effects (Year 2+)

Timing: As hub-satellite clusters expand to new regions

Tactics

  • Regional Pioneer Chapters: Organized meetups in each hub cluster region
  • Cross-corridor challenges: "Charge in 3 different regions this year"
  • Member-exclusive site previews: Pioneers get first charge at new locations
  • National brand partnerships: Expand beyond regional to nationwide perks
  • Trailblazer program maturity: 100-250 members nationwide, Executive Advisory Board

Goal

Self-sustaining referral engine, members = brand ambassadors

Success Metrics

Membership Health Metrics

Enrollment Metrics

  • Enrollment rate: % of unique sessions that sign up (target: 55%)
  • Paid conversion rate: % of Explorers upgrading to Voyager/Pioneer (target: 35% by Year 3)
  • Tier distribution: Mix of Explorer/Voyager/Pioneer/Trailblazer over time
  • Earned vs. purchased: % of paid members who earned vs. bought their tier

Retention Metrics

  • Annual renewal rate: % of paid members renewing (target: 70%)
  • Churn rate: % of paid members downgrading or cancelling
  • Trailblazer retention: % maintaining minimum 30 sessions/year (target: 95%+)
  • Tier stability: % maintaining or progressing tiers year-over-year

Engagement Metrics

  • Member NPS: Net Promoter Score among members (target: 75+)
  • App engagement: Daily/weekly/monthly active users
  • Challenge participation: % completing quarterly challenges
  • Referral participation: % of members referring at least 1 friend
  • Event attendance: % of eligible members attending Pioneer Rallies, Summits

Behavioral Impact Metrics

Session Frequency

  • Sessions per member per month (by tier)
  • Member vs. non-member session frequency ratio
  • Repeat visit rate: % returning within 30/60/90 days

Transaction Metrics

  • Average retail spend per session (members vs. non-members)
  • Attachment rate: % of charging sessions including retail purchase
  • Basket size by tier (Explorer vs. Voyager vs. Pioneer)
  • Dwell time by tier (longer dwell = more retail opportunity)

Acquisition Metrics

  • % of new members from referrals
  • Cost per acquisition (CPA) by channel
  • Referral conversion rate: % of referred leads who sign up
  • Time to first paid tier (how long do Explorers stay free?)

Financial Performance Metrics

Revenue Metrics

  • Monthly recurring revenue (MRR) from memberships
  • Annual recurring revenue (ARR) from memberships
  • Revenue per member (total: charging + retail + membership fee)
  • Membership revenue as % of total company revenue

Profitability Metrics

  • Contribution margin by tier (revenue - variable costs)
  • Customer lifetime value (LTV) by tier (5-year projection)
  • LTV:CAC ratio (target: 3:1 or higher)
  • Payback period: Time to recover acquisition cost (target: <12 months)
